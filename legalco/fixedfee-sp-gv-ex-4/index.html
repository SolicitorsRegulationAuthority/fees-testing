Glossary of Terms

Administrator

The person responsible for looking after the affairs of someone who has died without making a Will

Asset

Anything a person owns of value including property and land, investments or money

Beneficiary

A person who benefits on the death of another, either under a Will or on Intestacy

Estate

A term used to describe a persons assets and property

Executor

A person named in a Will to administer the deceased’s estate and distribute the estate in accordance with the terms of the Will

Grand of Letters of Administration

A document issued to a deceased’s administrator to authorise the administrator to deal with the estate

Grant of Probate

A document issued to the executors of a Will which will allow them to administer and distribute the estate in accordance with the terms of the Will

Inheritance Tax

A tax of the total value of the deceased’s estate

Asset

Anything a person owns of value including property and land, investments or money

Intestate/Intestacy

A phrase to describe someone who has died without making a valid Will and the subsequent administration of that persons estate

Testator/Testatrix

A person who has made a Will

Trust

Where assets are held or looked after by Trustees for the benefit of one of more persons

Trustee

A person or persons who look after and manage the Trust and Trust assets until such time as the Trust is brought to an end

Will

A written document outlining a persons last wishes to include the appointment of Executors, Guardians and the gift of money or assets to one of more beneficiaries.

Witness

One of the two persons who together will watch the Testator sign their Will and then sign in their presence to validate the Will.
